Landfill Leachate Treatment: Complete Guide for India

What is Leachate?

Leachate is the liquid that forms when water filters through waste in a landfill. It contains high concentrations of:

  • Organic matter (COD: 500-50,000 mg/L)
  • Ammonia nitrogen
  • Heavy metals
  • Chlorides
  • Total Dissolved Solids (TDS: 1,000-30,000 mg/L)

Leachate Characteristics by Age

Leachate composition changes as landfill ages:

  • Young landfill (<5 years): High organic acids, low ammonia, pH 4-6
  • Medium age (5-10 years): Methanogenic, stable, pH 7-8
  • Old landfill (>10 years): Low organics, high ammonia, pH 8-9

Leachate Treatment Technologies

1. Biological Treatment

Suitable for young leachate with high BOD/COD ratio. Methods include:

  • Activated sludge
  • Sequencing batch reactors (SBR)
  • MBR (Membrane Bioreactor)

2. Physicochemical Treatment

For mature leachate with low BOD/COD:

  • Coagulation-flocculation
  • Advanced oxidation (Ozone, Fenton)
  • Membrane processes (NF, RO)

Leachate Treatment in India - Challenges

  • Regulatory pressure: CPCB guidelines strict on landfill Leachate
  • Variable composition: Hard to standardize treatment
  • Cost: Treatment can cost ₹200-500 per cubic meter
  • Infrastructure: Many landfills lack proper collection/treatment
