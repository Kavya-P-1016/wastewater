Zero Liquid Discharge (ZLD) Systems: The Complete Guide for Indian Industries

What is Zero Liquid Discharge (ZLD)?

Zero Liquid Discharge (ZLD) is an advanced wastewater treatment technology that achieves 99%+ water recovery, leaving zero harmful discharge to the environment. For Indian industries facing stricter regulations from CPCB and state pollution control boards, ZLD has become not just an option but a mandate.

How Does ZLD Work?

A typical ZLD system combines multiple treatment stages:

  • Pre-treatment: Filtration, chemical treatment
  • Concentration: RO, NF, or evaporation to reduce volume
  • Evaporation: Thermal or non-thermal evaporation of remaining concentrate
  • Crystallization: Final drying of solids (optional)

Traditional vs Non-Thermal ZLD

Traditional ZLD systems rely on thermal evaporation (MED, MVR, multiple-effect evaporators) which have high energy costs. Non-thermal evaporation, like Asiatic Engineers' patented ATM system, uses mechanical atomization and natural evaporation - reducing CAPEX by up to 60% and OPEX by up to 70%.

ZLD Cost in India (2024)

The cost of a ZLD plant depends on:

  • Flow rate (liters per day)
  • Influent TDS levels
  • Required recovery rate
  • Technology used (thermal vs non-thermal)

Typical costs: ₹50 lakh - ₹5 crore for industrial ZLD systems in Gujarat. Non-thermal systems offer 40-70% lower operating costs compared to thermal alternatives.

Why Gujarat Industries Need ZLD
