ZLD Cost Comparison: Thermal vs Non-Thermal Evaporation | Asiatic Engineers

Choosing a Zero Liquid Discharge (ZLD) system is a major capital decision. This guide compares the two main evaporation technologies to help you make an informed choice.

Two Main Evaporation Technologies

1. Thermal Evaporation

Uses heat to vaporize water. Includes:

  • MVR (Mechanical Vapor Recompression) - Recovers vapor heat
  • MED (Multiple Effect Distillation) - Multiple stages of evaporation
  • Thermal Vapor Compression - Combines heat sources

2. Non-Thermal Evaporation

Uses mechanical atomization + natural evaporation:

  • Atomizing Spray - Fine water droplets
  • Forced Draft - Air circulation
  • Natural Evaporation - Solar/ambient

When to Choose Thermal

  • Limited space (compact required)
  • Very high volumes (>500 m³/day)
  • 24/7 continuous operation
  • Availability of cheap steam/heat

When to Choose Non-Thermal

  • Budget constraints
  • Lower volumes (<200 m³/day)
  • Intermittent operation
  • Want fastest ROI
  • No steam availability

Conclusion

For most Indian industries, non-thermal evaporation offers superior economics. The 60-70% lower operating costs translate to faster ROI and lower total cost of ownership. Thermal systems make sense only in specific high-volume, continuous-operation scenarios.
